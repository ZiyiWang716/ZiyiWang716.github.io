App Introcudtion

Coursera is an online learning platform that offers anyone, anywhere, access to online courses and degrees from leading universities and companies. Founded in 2012, Coursera has gained huge success with over 90 million registered users from over 200 countries. The average rating of course on Coursera is 4.7 out of 5 and 93% of Coursera learners say they would recommend the platform to their friends.

Probelm

With more and more people having easy access to internet, online learning has become a popular tool for people to reach their academic and career goals. Moreover, since pandemic, Coursera has seen a 450% increase in the number of enrollments in 2020 and the trend of virtual learning continues to grow in nowadays. However, among the 92 million students in Coursera, only 20% of them use mobile only and the majority of them has expressed that they have a much more good experience accessing Coursera through website than native app. while increasingly more people take advantage of their mobile phones and want to study on their phones utilizing the fragmented time during the day, Coursera would definitely serve their users better if they could improve their mobile app version.

Usability Testing

To get a better understanding of user behaviors, I conducted one-to-one usability test with 3 people who had known and registered for Coursera before but did not end up using it for some reasons. I hoped that by observing the using experience of these "disappointed users", I would be able to find some insights of how to improve the app.

Pre-Task Questions:

  • What online learning platrforms have you used before?
  • What would motivate you to use an online course tool?
  • What would be your goal for taking online courses?
  • Did you ever successfully accomplish an online course? Why or why not?

Tasks:

  • Find a course that you are interested in and enroll it
  • Watch one class video and take notes
  • Do a quiz
  • Join forum discussion

Ideation

Affinity Mapping

After the research stage, I compiled all the pain points identified and grouped similar ones. At last, the pain points were grouped into two groups of experience, one related to course finding and the other about course taking. Within the two groups, pain points were further divided into subsections containing different app pages. This mapping gave me a clear structure of what to improve and redesign in order to benefit users.

The Redesign

· Course Finding ·

1. Explore

Card Sorting

Since Explore is the place that users browse to find the course they want, the app could only achieve its success by capturing its users' taste. Therefore, I asked 8 participants to do a card sorting on how they would prioritize different categories of courses suggested on the Explore page and redesign the Explore page based on their preferences.

Design Outcome

2. Enroll

Button Testing

In the research stage, many users had expressed their confusions with the words Coursera had used. Therefore, to give the users a better experience enrolling courses, I decided to do a button testing for the CTA buttons in the course enroll page with some users so that confusions and mistakes could be avoided in the redesign.
