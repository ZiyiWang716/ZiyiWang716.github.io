    Landscapes were a beloved genre of woodblock prints. Japanese people had a deep spiritual love and respect for nature, relishing in its beauty. Waterfalls, for instance, were believed to be sacred within the animistic values of the Shinto religion, and were a frequent pilgrimage and subject matter in ukiyo-e. Mount Fuji was another popular character for ukiyo-e, due to its cultural and religious significance, as it was believed to be the source for immortality.
